# Whistle Civic Notice And Asset Attribution

Whistle is being developed as an open-source civic contribution. It is offered in appreciation of democratic participation, public-service leadership, citizen accountability, and the ongoing effort to move Tamil Nadu and other jurisdictions toward cleaner, more measurable, and more humane governance.

The name `Whistle` is also a tribute to whistleblowing as a healthy civic concept. In a good society, raising a whistle should not be seen only as accusation or conflict. It can be a constructive act: a citizen calling attention to what must be fixed so public life can transform for the better.

The project is built with the hope that citizens can raise issues with confidence, that government teams can act with clarity, and that public service can become more transparent, measurable, and humane. A corruption-free public life is not only a technology problem. It requires institutions, leadership, citizens, process discipline, and trust. Whistle is a software contribution toward that larger civic purpose.

The original Whistle code and documentation are released under the MIT License so that Tamil Nadu, other Indian states, civic technologists, public institutions, and other countries may study, adapt, improve, and reuse the approach where it serves the public good.

Whistle is unofficial. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, sponsored by, or authorized by the Government of Tamil Nadu, any Chief Minister's office, any political party, or any public figure unless a future deployment separately documents that authorization.

## Spirit Of Contribution

Whistle is created in good faith and in the best interest of citizens, the state, public institutions, and humanity. The intent is to support accountability, service delivery, citizen trust, and corruption-free governance.

If any wording, image, symbol, representation, or implementation detail is inaccurate, inappropriate, unauthorized, or hurtful, it is unintentional. We ask for understanding and welcome correction. Please notify the maintainers so the issue can be reviewed and fixed promptly.

## Open-Source Intent

The project is open source because civic infrastructure improves when it can be inspected, debated, adapted, and improved. Whistle is meant to be a starting point, not a final claim of perfection.

Anyone adapting this work should preserve the same public-interest spirit:

- Protect citizen identity and dignity.
- Keep sensitive complaints safe.
- Avoid using public dashboards to expose private data.
- Preserve auditability and accountability.
- Make roles, SLAs, and escalation paths clear.
- Respect the rights of governments, public figures, photographers, designers, and asset owners.
