# Code Of Conduct

Whistle is civic infrastructure software. Project discussion must stay useful, respectful, and grounded in the public-interest goals of privacy, safety, accountability, and maintainability.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct, factual, and constructive.
- Critique code, designs, documents, and decisions without personal attacks.
- Protect citizen dignity, sensitive complaints, private evidence, and contributor safety.
- Respect copyright, trademark, publicity, and official-use rules for assets and identity material.
- Assume public issue threads are permanent and avoid posting private operational details.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, abuse, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private citizen data, protected complaint details, secrets, credentials, OTPs, private URLs, or sensitive evidence.
- Posting exploitable security details in public issues.
- Misrepresenting Whistle as officially endorsed by any government, party, department, public figure, or institution without documented approval.
- Adding unlicensed marks, portraits, photos, seals, or official-looking assets to the public repository.

## Reporting

Report conduct problems to the maintainers through a private channel. If the report involves a security vulnerability, follow `SECURITY.md` and do not open a public issue with exploit details.

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content that creates safety, privacy, legal, or security risk. Repeated or serious violations may lead to blocked participation.
